The Impact of Social Media on Politics

Society’s widespread internet use has made the political environment as hard as ever to navigate through. What it has done essentially is expose the bias and poor behavior of incompetent and corrupt political figures and platforming them to the nation’s detriment.

Symbolizing a concerningly increasing public ignorance and normalization of irresponsible and irredeemable behavior. It is truly a demonstration of the horrors of improper misuse of constitutional civil liberties.

The Role of Donald J. Trump and Elon Musk

Donald J. Trump and Elon Musk play one of the most significant parts in the nation’s current political climate. Their political influence is supported and backed by half the nation’s population which means that they have an already well established presence in the internet.

Elon Musk, as a matter of fact is the richest man in the world and has a lot of money, connections, and resources in his disposal, he even owns one the biggest social media platforms for online political discourse, “X”, formally known as “Twitter”. However, he also is the head of a US government agency, Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE), a post that President Donald J Trump, the president of the United States who happened to also be a multi-billionaire, gave to him.

Irresponsible Use of Civil Liberties

Together, both of these politically powerful men have used their civil liberties irresponsibly online with the spread of misinformation, the hyperbolic use of language, and the supporting of irresponsible behavioral actions.

Donald Trump has made over 30,000 misleading claims since his first term of president in 2016, most of these claims took place in online social media platforms he had accounts to and were intended to obtain as much attention as they could possibly gather.

He even attacked people’s faith in government by spreading false and misleading claims of race discrimination by public health authorities during the Covid-19 Pandemic. Furthermore, he abused the social power he had online by making false claims on the nation’s ability to withhold free and fair elections, many of his claims centering around the argument that election results were being rigged to support his political enemies.

Elon Musk's Actions

Elon Musk on the other hand, mimics Donald Trump’s behavior online to a significant extent, and what he also likes to do is openly support him online, posting memes defending Trump from criticism from his political enemies.

At one point, he even abused his position as owner of X to help kill a spending bill from Congress, deceivingly misrepresenting the contents of the proposed bill online to sway public opinion against it.

But in addition to all of this, he would become notorious for making Nazi salutes in front of the presidential seal of the United States of America during Trump’s inauguration and making Nazi jokes online following the entire incident.

Consequences and Implications

However, despite these red flags of both characters, portraying their irresponsible misuse of their online presence and their followers, they haven’t received any real consequences for their actions.

Donald Trump was able to win back the presidency in 2024 and Elon Musk was given an advisory position over DOGE, giving him power in managing and overseeing the nation’s federal budget.

The politicalized internet landscape of today has given way for atrocious individuals to gain undeserved political power. It has allowed for them to get into political offices in which they have no expertise nor credentials over and thus leaves them with power to exploit to favor themselves.

Even if there are arguments that suggest that people still have the ability to hold undesirable lawmakers accountable for bad actions, it doesn’t really seem to be the case for our nation’s currently most influential political leaders.
